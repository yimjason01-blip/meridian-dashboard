How to read this chart

X-axis — Risk Identified: The total magnitude of health risk made visible to the patient that would not have been visible through the benchmark. Measured as estimated cumulative relative risk reduction available if all identified risks are addressed. Further right = more risk was surfaced.

Y-axis — Risk Addressed: The expected reduction in risk from actions the patient actually took (or is on track to take). Further up = more identified risk was converted into real-world action.

The diagonal line represents perfect conversion — every risk identified is fully acted on. Points below the line represent unrealized potential.

The distance between the PCP point and Meridian's point is the value Meridian created.

Case study estimates

  • PCP: Identifies borderline LDL and overdue colonoscopy. Estimated addressable risk reduction ~15-20% (statin if started, colonoscopy screening benefit). Likely action conversion ~50%. Plots near lower-left.
  • Meridian: Identifies ATM-driven cancer risks, Lp(a) cardiovascular reclassification, bradycardia, and guideline-based screenings. Estimated addressable risk reduction ~55-65% across CV, cancer, and vitality domains. Observed action conversion ~67% (4 of 6 priorities). Plots upper-right.
  • Meridian (potential): If cardiac workup and fresh lipid panel are completed, conversion rises to ~90%+. Shown as the hollow point.

What the gap represents

The shaded area between the two points is not just "more findings." It represents risk that was invisible to the PCP — and would have remained invisible indefinitely — that Meridian made visible and actionable. For this patient, that includes pancreatic cancer surveillance, cardiovascular risk reclassification, and genetic counseling for his daughter.

Zero state: If Meridian found nothing the PCP wouldn't find, and the patient acted on nothing more, both points would overlap. The gap would be zero.
