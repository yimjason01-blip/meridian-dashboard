What this is saying

The gray line is the hidden truth.

Your underlying physiologic reserve usually changes gradually. That’s the part driven by fitness, metabolic health, vascular load, cognition, muscle, and resilience.

The navy line is what life feels like.

People experience life in chunks: “I was fine.” “Then something happened.” “I got some of it back.” “Then another thing happened.” That’s why the lived path is jagged.

The Meridian opportunity

Keep the hidden baseline higher for longer. Reduce the odds and severity of big drops. Improve the rebound after setbacks.

Before the event: detect risk earlier and delay the drop.
After the event: accelerate recovery and preserve more function.
Across the whole life: compress the years spent dependent.
Not a staircase only The baseline is still continuous. The discrete events are layered on top of it.
Not pure randomness Those step-downs are often made more or less likely by the baseline you’ve built.
Best pitch implication Meridian is not just adding years. It is protecting the years where life still feels like your life.
