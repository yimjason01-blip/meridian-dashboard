Where Meridian fits.

🔄

Continuous vs. Snapshot

Most options give you one day of testing, then silence until next year. Meridian combines twice-yearly labs with daily wearable data for a picture that updates continuously — not just when you visit a clinic.

🧬

Everything Analyzed Together

Your wearable data, genetics, bloodwork, and family history live in separate silos today. Meridian is the first service that generates its own clinical data AND integrates it with your other sources into one unified risk model.

🎯

Physician-Prescribed Priorities, Not Data Dumps

A 50-page report or a dashboard of numbers isn't a plan. Your Meridian physician reviews the risk model and prescribes your #1 priority — why it outranks everything else and exactly what to do about it.

⚖️

Honest Tradeoffs

Meridian doesn't replace your doctor or a Fountain Life MRI. It doesn't do imaging or in-person exams. What it does is connect data sources that nobody else connects, model risks nobody else models, and give your physician the tools to prescribe what actually matters most.

"How does this compare to…?"

"I already have a doctor — why do I need this?"
Your PCP is essential — Meridian doesn't replace them. But a standard annual physical with basic labs checks ~15 markers and has limited time to connect dots across your genetics, wearable trends, and family history. Meridian generates deeper data (2× annual labs + genetic sequencing), synthesizes it with your wearable data, and your Meridian physician uses that to prescribe a prioritized plan. You walk into your PCP visit knowing exactly what to discuss — because your Meridian physician already told you.
"How is this different from Fountain Life?"
Fountain Life offers a best-in-class clinical experience — full-body MRI, DEXA, genomics, physician consult — that Meridian can't replicate. For that one day, they're unmatched. The gap is the other 364 days. Meridian provides continuous monitoring between those visits and integrates Fountain Life's data with your daily wearable trends and ongoing lab results. For someone who can afford both, they're complementary. For most people, Meridian delivers 80% of the insight at a fraction of the cost.
"What about Function Health? They test 160 biomarkers for $30/month."
Function is excellent at what it does — broad, affordable lab testing with good trend tracking. If you only want bloodwork, Function is hard to beat on value. Where Meridian differs: it includes genetic sequencing, integrates wearable data, layers in family history, and models risk across cardiovascular, cancer, and cognitive domains. Function tells you your ApoB is 96. Your Meridian physician tells you what that means given your genetics, your Lp(a), and your training frequency — and prescribes the highest-impact action.
"Human Longevity does genomics + AI — same thing?"
Similar ambition, different model. Human Longevity offers world-class genome sequencing and full-body MRI with physician interpretation — genuine clinical depth that Meridian doesn't match on imaging. But it's a $5K annual visit in San Diego, and between visits there's no ongoing monitoring. Meridian fills the gap: continuous wearable integration, twice-yearly labs, and a risk model that evolves as new data comes in.
"Why not just get a concierge doctor?"
A concierge doctor gives you something Meridian can't: a trusted human relationship, in-person exams, and clinical judgment honed over years. That has real value. Where concierge falls short is data integration — even a great physician can't continuously monitor your wearable trends, cross-reference your genome, and model multi-domain risk at scale. Meridian augments the concierge relationship; it doesn't replace it.
"Superpower does blood tests + AI for $17/month — why pay 10× more?"
Superpower is a well-designed blood testing app at an accessible price — good entry point for anyone curious about their biomarkers. The difference: Superpower tests once per year (vs. Meridian's twice), doesn't include genetics, has limited wearable integration, and its AI recommendations are primarily supplement-focused. Meridian includes clinical-grade genetic sequencing, models cross-domain risk, and your physician prescribes prioritized clinical actions — not supplement suggestions. Different depth for different needs.
"Didn't Forward Health try this and fail?"
Forward tried to replace the doctor with an AI kiosk — $650M raised, shut down in 2024. That's a fundamentally different bet. Meridian doesn't replace anyone. It's an intelligence layer that generates clinical data (labs + genetics), integrates it with your wearables, and makes it actionable. No hardware, no clinics, no replacing the physician relationship. Forward proved demand exists for AI-powered health insights. The delivery model was the problem.
