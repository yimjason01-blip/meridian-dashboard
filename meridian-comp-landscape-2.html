The distribution gap.

🏥

Mayo / Cleveland Clinic

Sell direct to corporate HR as an executive perk. 5-10 C-suite leaders per company. $5K-$10K per head. No broker channel, no voluntary benefits, no carrier involvement. Distribution = brand prestige + corporate sales team.

🤝

Meridian

Sells through brokers as a voluntary benefit. Accessible to the broader executive/professional tier — directors, VPs, managers — not just the C-suite. Distributed through the same carrier salesforce that sells CI, disability, and life. Scales with every enrollment, not one corporate deal at a time.

📊

Different Buyers, Different Budgets

Mayo sells to the CFO who signs a $50K check for 5 execs. Meridian sells to the benefits committee that adds a $200/mo voluntary option for 200 professionals. Same annual spend to the employer — 10× the covered lives.

🎯

Same Budget, More Coverage

A company spending $50K on Mayo covers 5 executives once a year. That same $50K on Meridian covers 20 professionals year-round with continuous monitoring. Same benefits budget, same decision-maker — radically different reach and frequency.
